Preparing for a Job Interview in 5 Easy Steps.


Charles Loxton Jan 14, 2023.
Software Engineer

"A cover letter is your chance to explain why you're genuinely interested in the company and the job."
The Muse

The job interview process can be intimidating, but with preparation and practice, you can make sure your next interview lands you that job! Here are five steps to help you prepare for a successful job interview

1. Research the Employer:

Before your interview, take the time to learn as much as you can about the company you’re interviewing with. This will show the interviewer that you’re genuinely interested in the company and that you’ve taken the time to become familiar with their work. Take a look at their website, familiarize yourself with their values, and check out their social media accounts. This will help you form thoughtful questions about the company and show that you’ve put in the effort to truly understand their vision and mission.

2. Re-read the Job Description:

Before the interview, take a few minutes to re-read the job description. Take note of the skills, experience, and qualifications they’re looking for and focus on how your own experience and qualifications can help fulfill their needs. Pay attention to the adjectives they use to describe the ideal candidate and use those adjectives in your answers to behavioral questions.

3. Prepare Answers to Common Questions:

One of the best ways to prepare for your job interview is to look up and memorise pre-rehearsed answers to common questions. Before your interview, take some time to research questions that employers commonly tend to ask. Write a 50-100 word answer for each one and practice saying them out loud.

This will help you feel more prepared and confident when the time comes for your interview. Don’t worry if you can’t remember the answers word for word—the interviewer have no idea what you were meant to say.

4. Study Your Resume and Cover Letter:

  1. It’s natural to feel a little nervous when you’re asked to summarize your work history at the beginning of the interview. To make sure you’re prepared, take some time beforehand to study your cover letter and internalize the information listed on your resume.

  2. Practice talking about yourself out loud in a friendly, natural, and confident manner so that you don’t sound like you’re reciting a list.

5. Practice Your Interview:

Before your interview, practice with a friend or family member. Have them ask you common questions and practice your answers. If you’re feeling really nervous, practice with a mirror or record yourself. This will help you become comfortable with the questions and give you the confidence you need to make a good impression.

By following these 5 steps, you’ll find yourself more confident and relaxed come the day of the interview. Remember, you were moved on in the process for a reason. If the interviewer wasn’t impressed by the skills listed on your resume and cover letter, you wouldn’t have been invited for an interview.
